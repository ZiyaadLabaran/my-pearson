ABOUT US

Welcome to the official tourism website of Ghana, brought to you by the Ministry of Tourism, Arts, and Culture (MoTAC). As part of our commitment to promoting sustainable tourism, we invite you to explore the rich tapestry of experiences that our beautiful country has to offer. Ghana is known for its vibrant culture, breathtaking landscapes, and warm hospitality. With the Ghana Tourism Authority's latest initiative, we aim to enhance visitor experiences while preserving our natural and cultural heritage. This platform serves as your gateway to uncovering the diverse attractions, from historic sites and artistic expressions to eco-friendly adventures that respect our environment.

Featured Tourist Sites

Mole National Park

Mole National Park is located in the northern region of Ghana, near the town of Damongo in the West Gonja District. It is the largest national park in Ghana, covering an area of approximately 4,577 square kilometers (1,768 square miles).

Larabanga Ancient Mosque

The Larabanga Mosque is one of the most iconic landmarks in Ghana, known for its historical and architectural significance. Located in the village of Larabanga in the northern part of Ghana, near Mole National Park, the mosque is considered one of the oldest in West Africa and is a key cultural and religious site.

Salaga Slave Market

The Salaga Slave Trade refers to the significant role that the town of Salaga, located in the Northern Region of Ghana, played in the transatlantic slave trade during the 18th and 19th centuries.

Nalerigu Slave Defence Wall

The Nalerigu Defense Wall, also known as the Nalerigu Fort, is a historical site located in Nalerigu, a town in the North East Region of Ghana. It is one of the few remaining forts from the colonial era in Ghana and holds significant historical importance, particularly in the context of the slave trade and the British colonial administration.

Kakum National Park

Kakum National Park is one of the most famous and biodiverse national parks in Ghana, located in the Central Region of the country, near the town of Cape Coast. Established in 1992, the park is known for its lush rainforests, rich biodiversity, and the famous Canopy Walkway, which offers a unique perspective of the forest's upper layers.

Independence Square

Independence Square, also known as Black Star Square, is one of the most significant landmarks in Accra, the capital city of Ghana. It is a major symbol of Ghana’s independence and a prominent site for national celebrations, especially for events such as Independence Day, state functions, and official ceremonies.

Dance and Festivals

Damba Festival

The Damba Festival celebrates the founding of the Dagomba kingdom and the achievements of its ancestors. It is a way of honoring the rich cultural heritage, customs, and traditions of the Dagombas.

Fire Festival (Bugum Festival)

The Fire Festival is believed to have originated from the ancient traditions of the Dagombas. It is traditionally celebrated to mark the return of the people from hunting expeditions, as well as the early days of the Dagomba kingdom's establishment.

Bakatue Festival

The Bakatue Festival is believed to have originated centuries ago when the ancestors of Elmina settlers first arrived and established the town. The festival marks the beginning of the fishing season, as well as the town's connection to the sea.

Kundum Festival

Kundum Festival is Celebrated by the Ahanta and Nzema people of the western region of Ghana.It is Celebrated to thank God for The Abandance of food at the time of the hestival period of the area.

Homowo Festival

It is celebrated by theGa people of Greather Accra to commemorate the end of a historic famine.it invoves drumming ,dancing,and the sprinkling of "Kpokpoi"(traditional food).

Aboakyiri Festival

The Aboakyiri festival is celebrated by the people of Winneba in the Central Region.it involves the a deer-hunting competition to honor the migration of the Simpa People.

Food of Northern Ghana

T.Z

Tuo Zaafi (often abbreviated as TZ) is a staple meal that is both nutritious and satisfying. It is a starchy dish that is typically eaten by the people in northern Ghana and is enjoyed by many across the country.

Wasawasa

Wasawasa is a type of fermented corn dough dish that is made by combining fermented cornmeal and cassava dough.

Tubani

Tubani is a dish made from ground beans (typically black-eyed beans) that are cooked into a thick, smooth dough-like consistency. The beans give Tubani its rich and hearty texture, making it a fulfilling dish.

Fufu

It is a staple dish in many West and Central African countries. Fufu is a traditional dish made from boiled and pounded starchy vegetables,fruits,or grains.

Banku

It is a traditional fermented corn dough dish thatis popular in Northern Ghana particularly among the the Dagomba and Gonja people.Banku is oftern served with a spicy sauce or peper.

Akpele

Akpele is a traditional fermented corn dough dish that's popular in Ghana,particularly among the Ewe people. Akpele is similar to Banku, but its is made with a different type of corn and a slightly differnt texture and flavour.
